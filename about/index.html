Intro

I have over 8 years of experience as a software engineer, and have worked on both small startups and large organisations. While I’m a proficient full-stack developer, my expertise is in building scalable backend services (API services, stream processing, and async mechanisms).

I love building things. While hard engineering problems are often intrinsically fun to tackle, I’m most attracted to solving real customer problems with a business justification. I’m looking for a senior individual contributor role where I can take on on collaborative team leadership responsibilites, and gain experience with architecture and project management.

Experience

I’ve started my career at a small startup (2013-2017), where I had the pleasure to work on really interesting projects. Most notable:

  • Smartmart - fully-automated store that allows customers to shop from the comfort of their driver’s seat. On Smartmart I worked on both the backend services and also on the devices integration that allowed the automation.

The project was build on an actor model using Akka to allow for scallability and complete remote controll over integrated devices.

The work that I’m most proud was pitching and integrating with WebRTC that allow clients to call operators for help, when WebRTC was still and experimental feature and Google and Cisco were fighting over the codec that should be used.

  • Quickboard a projet that proposes a new way of training that helps athletes to track their progress. Quickboard was my first project where I did the arhitecture and oversee its release to production. Some of the challenges faced:

    • creating a sync protocol that allowed from the data to be sent form multiple devices and multiple athletes at the same time over slow internet connection.

    • creating meaningfull analytics for athletes and build an intuitive interface that allowed coaches to build their own.

    • building a reliable way for releasing new firmware updated to the boards over Bluetooth.

After Quickboard I’ve joined Fitbit (2017-now) and I can say that I’m part of Google since the Fitbit acquisition (01/FEB/2021).

During my time in Fibit I worked on all type of projects, from scalling our infrastructure to handle incoming traffic, to building the architecture for new services, implementing it and getting them into production, to validating, improving and deploying the algorithms that the research team created, while making sure that everything has monitoring/alerts. But I can’t say that I did all this by myself, I am part of a great team that is constantly improving and thriving for greater challenges.

Fitbit taught me the importance of writting good tests, why a good documentation matters and why nothing should be released if it doesn’t have monitoring and alerts, and Google took that knowledge to the next level.
